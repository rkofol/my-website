Impressionism

Impressionism is a style of painting that became popular in the late 19th century. Impressionist artists used small, visible brush strokes to represent form, unblended color, and natural light. This art movement was ground breaking at the time and continues to influence the art world today.

    Key Artists (Read More)

  • Claude Monet
  • Pierre-Auguste Renoir
  • Edgar Degas

    Characteristics

  • Quick, loose brush strokes
  • "En plein air" (Painting outdoors)
  • Scenes of middle class leisure, everyday life, and landscapes
  • Accurate depiction of lighting
  • Influence from Japanese prints and photography
